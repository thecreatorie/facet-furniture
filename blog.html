Exploring Furniture Finishes

Furniture finishes are paramount in defining the final appearance and durability of a piece. They can magnify the inherent beauty of wood, offer protection, and dictate the style of a room. At Facet Furniture, finishes are our forte.

What is a furniture finish?

Furniture finishes are protective layers applied to the surface of wooden pieces, enhancing their aesthetics and durability. More than just a superficial coating, they are specially formulated to bond with the wood, ensuring longevity and resistance against external elements.

Why finishes matter

Enhanced aesthetics: Finishes can elevate the visual appeal of wooden furniture, highlighting its grain and adding depth to its natural color.

Protection: They act as a shield against moisture, UV rays, scratches, and everyday wear and tear, ensuring your furniture stands the test of time.

Maintenance: Finished furniture is easier to clean and less susceptible to staining compared to untreated wood.

How to choose the right finish

Understand the ambiance: Think about the mood and style you want to convey. For a clean, modern feel, the Ghost White finish might be your go-to. If you're leaning towards a traditional warm tone, the Pure Living Finish might be more your style.

Consider your needs: For furniture pieces that will face daily wear and tear, opt for finishes that offer heightened protection. On every Facet Furniture piece there are a wide variety of finishes to choose from, which get filtered as you select the type of wood you'd like to use on your project.

Consult with experts: Our team is always ready to guide you through our range of finishes to find the perfect fit for your home. You can request a sample kit by contacting us at info@facetfurniture.com.

Finishes are the unsung heroes of furniture crafting. They encapsulate the essence, protect the core, and determine the lifespan of a piece. At Facet Furniture, our curated range of finishes is a testament to our commitment to quality, style, and longevity.

How To Design Custom Furniture For Your Home

The idea of custom furniture is appealing for many reasons. Whether you're looking to fill a specific space, achieve a unique aesthetic, or own a piece that tells a story, custom furniture is the answer.

Understand the purpose

Before diving into design details, consider what purpose your furniture will serve. Is it a functional piece like a dining table or a decorative accent like a coffee table?

Choose your wood

White Oak: Known for its durability and fine grain, ideal for contemporary designs. Walnut: Rich, dark finish with an elegant grain pattern, perfect for classic, timeless pieces. Reclaimed Oak: For an eco-friendly, rustic look with rich history.

Select a finish

From the pale allure of Ghost White to the rich hues of Natural Walnut or the chic Shade Pure Living, the finish can dramatically change the piece's appearance. Think about the ambiance you want to create.

Size and shape

Measure your space. Ensure the furniture piece fits perfectly in its intended location. Consider movement around the piece — you don't want your new table blocking pathways.

Timeline and budget

Custom pieces take time. Factor in design approvals, material sourcing, and crafting. It's worth the wait for a unique piece crafted to perfection. Custom furniture can be more expensive than off-the-shelf pieces due to the specialized work involved. Set a budget but be flexible — it's a long-term investment.

Collaborate with experts

At Facet Furniture, we encourage our clients to work closely with our designers. They can guide you on latest trends, timeless designs, and the technicalities of furniture making. Custom furniture is more than just a piece of decor — it's an expression of your personal style, a tale of craftsmanship, and a cherished heirloom for future generations.

Decoding Wood: Walnut, White Oak, and Reclaimed Oak

At the heart of custom furniture lies the choice of material, particularly the type of wood. Three popular options we utilize here at Facet Furniture are Walnut, White Oak, and Reclaimed Oak.

Walnut

Walnut boasts a rich, deep hue ranging from dark brown to a light pale brown, often with contrasting darker streaks. The wood has a smooth, fine texture with a natural lustrous surface. The warm tones provide an elegant and sophisticated touch, making it a favorite for luxury interiors. Walnut is a hard, dense wood resistant to warping and shrinking, ideal for pieces that need to withstand wear.

White Oak

White Oak is characterized by its light to medium brown color with a slightly golden hue. It's strong, heavy, and highly durable with excellent moisture resistance. Its prominent ray fleck patterns when quartersawn make it versatile across both modern and traditional styles. While generally more affordable than walnut, top-grade white oak can still come with a significant price tag.

Reclaimed European Oak

Reclaimed Oak is salvaged from older structures and repurposed for new furniture. It often boasts a rustic appearance with notable wear, nail holes, and other marks that tell its story. Using reclaimed wood means fewer trees are cut down, contributing to environmental conservation. Each piece has its own history, ensuring no two pieces of furniture are alike. Old-growth oak used in historical buildings tends to be denser and stronger than new oak.

Selecting the perfect wood for your custom furniture is a crucial step in ensuring longevity, aesthetics, and overall satisfaction. Whatever you choose, remember that with proper care and maintenance, your custom furniture piece can serve as a cherished asset for years to come.
